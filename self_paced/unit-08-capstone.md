# Unit 8: Capstone Portfolio

Time: 1 hour

Navigation: [Track home](README.md) | Previous: [Unit 7](unit-07-handoffs-lessons-repo-stewardship.md)

Portfolio section: Unit 8

## Goal

Build a final Codex-ready task package grounded in your own work or in a synthetic training scenario.

## Anchored Experience

The capstone brings together the full pattern:

1. Choose a task.
2. Name local evidence.
3. Set privacy and source boundaries.
4. Define the output.
5. Verify the work.
6. Leave a handoff.

## Why This Matters

This is where the tutorial becomes your operating pattern. Instead of leaving with isolated exercises, you leave with a complete Codex-ready task package you can adapt to real work.

## Capstone Prompt Template

```text
You are working in [local folder or repo].
First inspect [files/docs/scripts].
Goal: [specific task].
Audience: [who will use the output].
Source boundary: [local files, repo docs, approved systems].
Privacy boundary: [aggregate-only, sanitized, internal, or restricted].
Output: [table, memo, report, script, handoff, review].
Verification: [checks, calculations, source audit, tests, or manual review].
Do not assume: [unknowns, identity matches, current status, row-level sharing, live-system access].
Close out with sources used, what changed, what was verified, and what remains uncertain.
```

## Deliverable

A completed [portfolio-template.md](portfolio-template.md).

## Finish Line

You are done when the portfolio feels like something you could hand to a colleague and say, "Here is the task, the evidence, the boundary, the check, and the next step." That is the payoff of the full tutorial.

## Self-Check

- Does the capstone name source evidence?
- Does it include a privacy boundary?
- Does it include what Codex should not assume?
- Does it specify verification?
- Could another person continue from the handoff?
